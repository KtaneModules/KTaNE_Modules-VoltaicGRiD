On the Subject of Broken Calculator

Even a broken clock is right twice a day.

A seemingly normal-looking, 10-digit calculator with an issue or two. Otherwise, perfectly functional, and useful for doing on-bomb calculations

Identifying the issue

During bomb initialization, the calculator displays a series of simple, pre-algebraic equations, and what the calculator says it equals. Of these calculations, at least one is wrong, leading to an incorrect equals value.

The following arithmetic operations are possible on the calculator, but the calculator is unable to perform that action.

Addition, Subtraction, Multiplication, Division, Modulus

Fixing the calculator

After identifying the above arithmetic operation that is broken, identify the "reset" equation(s) below and input them to repair the calculator's functionality.

Input the equation correctly, then tap the equals button to allow for a forced equals input, where the value of the equation can be put in. Inputting the wrong forced-reset value will incure a strike.

Variable values below must be calculated, before inputting their numerical value into the Broken Calculator.

If a voltage meter is present on the bomb, divide all non-variable numbers in the equations below by the voltage specified.
